## Current Multi-Channel Tracking

### What's Available Now

While dedicated channel-specific analytics are in development, you can currently track multi-channel performance through:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Combined channel metrics">
    **Current Analytics Include**:
    - **Total Visits**: Combined LINK clicks and QR scans
    - **Click Count**: Number of LINK channel interactions
    - **Scan Count**: Number of QR channel interactions
    - **Overall Performance**: Total engagement across both channels
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Manual channel analysis">
    **Current Workaround**:
    - Use tag-based filtering to separate channel-focused campaigns
    - Create separate touchpoints for LINK-only vs QR-only testing
    - Monitor performance trends for different deployment strategies
    - Track referrer data to understand channel context
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Strategic Channel Planning

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="LINK Channel Strategy" icon="link" color="#10B981">

## Preparing for Channel Analytics

### Optimization Strategies for Current Setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Organize touchpoints by channel focus">
    **Tag-Based Organization**:
    - Create tags like "link-focused", "qr-focused", or "multi-channel"
    - Use consistent tagging for future channel analysis
    - Plan touchpoint deployment with channel analytics in mind
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Track channel context manually">
    **Current Tracking Methods**:
    - Note where QR codes are physically deployed
    - Track which platforms are used for LINK sharing
    - Document campaign strategies for each channel
    - Monitor referrer data for channel context clues
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Plan for future analysis">
    **Preparation for Advanced Analytics**:
    - Maintain consistent naming conventions
    - Document channel deployment strategies
    - Plan A/B testing approaches for channel comparison
    - Consider how channel data will inform future campaigns
  </Step>
</Steps>
